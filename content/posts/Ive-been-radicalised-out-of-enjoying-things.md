I open a video from a friend.

The pilot announces that if the passengers would look out of the right-side window they would see something pretty special.

The Artemis II rocket launches from Florida.

And I cannot enjoy it.

I always liked space, and physics. I studied it until I was 18, doing very poorly in the exam, but being basically wide-eyed and interested. And I really like that episode of Mad Men.

Then at 30 I had a conversation that would change how I see basically everything.

One of my great life goals, ambitious but probably unachievable, was to have code that I write sent up to space. Among some other less-selfish things, some code that passed or helped pass the boundaries of the planet and would fly amongst the stars.

“Why would that be good?” they said.

“I just think it's amazing going to space." I said.

“Why do we go though?” they said.

“To understand, we don't really know how gravity works, for example, and by going to other planets, we can find out things.”

“But why? Do we need to know how gravity works?”

“Well we could go live up there. The planet's dying.”

“But we know why the planet's dying. We could fix that. But we don't. So going to another planet is just…”

The word hung in the air for a while.

“Colonialism.”

We'd be British-Empiring the solar system, and leaving our planet to rot.

The same is true of ‘experts’ saying AI will find the solve for climate change. We know the fix for climate change. We've known that for decades. Can't enjoy that either.

Books, the same. “Did you know he was an antisemite, a racist or a bigot.”

Music, movies, television, nothing is free of guilt.
